# Huáng Méi Guī (黄玫瑰, huáng méiguī)

**Huáng Méi Guī (黄玫瑰, huáng méiguī)**
![Хуан Мэй Гуй (黄玫瑰, huáng méiguī)](images/Хуан%20Мэй%20Гуй%20(黄玫瑰,%20Huáng%20Méiguī).png)

![Хуан Мэй Гуй (黄玫瑰, huáng méiguī)-1](images/Хуан%20Мэй%20Гуй%20(黄玫瑰,%20Huáng%20Méiguī)-1.png)

Huang Mei Gui ("Yellow Rose") is a young but already recognized Wuyi rock oolong, created at the intersection of modern breeding and centuries-old yancha traditions. Its calling card is a bright, natural rose aroma that emerges without any floral additives, exclusively through the genetics of the cultivar and masterful processing. Despite the variety's registration only in 2002, Huang Mei Gui quickly took its place among premium rock oolongs and became a symbol of innovation in Wuyi's tea industry.

---

**1. Classification and Origin:**

- **Type:** Oolong (turquoise tea). Rock tea (岩茶, yán chá). Oxidation level — medium (approximately 45%), roasting — light to medium.
- **Category:** Premium Wuyi oolongs.
- **Origin:** China, Fújiàn Province (福建), Wǔyí Mountains (武夷山), included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Main plantations are concentrated in Bànyán village (半岩) and adjacent areas at an altitude of 600–900 m a.s.l.
- **Cultivar:** Hybrid developed in 2002 by the Tea Research Institute of the Fujian Academy of Agricultural Sciences (jointly with the Wuyi Tea Culture Institute). Parent forms: Huáng Guǎn Yìn (黄观音, huáng guānyīn) × Huáng Dàn (黄旦, huáng dàn). First commercial harvest obtained in 2013 — 11 years after creating the hybrid.

---

**2. History and Cultural Significance:**

Development of the variety began in the late 20th century with the goal of expanding the assortment of Wuyi rock teas beyond the classic Da Hong Pao and Shui Xian, creating a cultivar with refined aromatics and increased resistance to the local climate. Registration took place in 2002, but the path from laboratory to commercial field took more than ten years.

The name "Huang Mei Gui" (黄玫瑰 — "Yellow Rose") reflects two key characteristics: the dominant rose aroma of the liquor and the golden-amber color of the brew. It is important to emphasize: this is not a flavored tea — the characteristic bouquet is formed exclusively by the properties of the leaf itself and processing technology.

Huang Mei Gui quickly gained recognition: in 2019 it took second place in the "Best Aroma" category at the international Tea Masters Cup competition. The tea is exported to many countries as a premium variety and is one of the few Wuyi oolongs with halal certification, which opens markets in Muslim countries.

---

**3. Botanical Description and Raw Material:**

- **Cultivar:** *Camellia sinensis* var. *sinensis*, hybrid Huang Guan Yin × Huang Dan. Medium-height bushes (1–1.8 m) with vertical shoot growth.
- **Leaves:** Large, elliptical in shape, 8–12 cm long and 3–4 cm wide, with a glossy dark green surface, pronounced venation and serrated edge.
- **Harvest:** Hand-picked, in April–May. Standard — bud and two upper leaves (一芽二叶). Spring first flush is especially valued. After picking, leaves undergo strict selection by size, color and elasticity.
- **Raw material requirements:** Only high-quality raw material from certified Wuyi plantations. To strengthen the root system and depth of flavor, old tree-like bushes over 30 years old are sometimes used.

---

**4. Terroir and Cultivation:**

- **Region:** Wuyi Mountains, northwest Fujian. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Main location — Bànyán village (半岩), terraced plantations between rock formations.
- **Altitude:** 600–900 m a.s.l.
- **Geology and soils:** Volcanic-sedimentary rocks, predominantly quartz sandstones rich in iron oxides. Acidic rocky soils with good drainage give the tea its characteristic "rock rhyme" (岩韵, yán yùn).
- **Climate:** Subtropical monsoon. Average annual temperature ~18°C, precipitation ~2000 mm/year, daily temperature fluctuations ~10°C. Fog — more than 100–150 days per year, slowing leaf growth and promoting accumulation of aromatic substances.
- **Hydrology:** Plantations are fed by spring water.
- **Agricultural techniques:** Biodynamic principles are often applied; pesticide ban, organic fertilizers. Export batches may have EU Organic, USDA Organic certificates.

---

**5. Production Technology:**

The technology follows the canons of Wuyi yancha with emphasis on preserving floral aromatics:

1. **Picking (采摘, cǎi zhāi):** Hand-picked, bud + two leaves.
2. **Withering (萎凋, wěidiāo):** First in the sun (~1 hour), then indoors with controlled ventilation (~8 hours). Loss of ~30% moisture.
3. **Shaking and bruising (摇青, yáo qīng):** Periodic shaking and bruising by hand or in bamboo drums. Damage to leaf edges activates oxidation. Temperature ~25°C, humidity >85%.
4. **Oxidation (发酵, fājiào):** Controlled oxidation to ~45%. The master is guided by changes in aroma and leaf appearance.
5. **Kill-green (杀青, shā qīng):** Quick pan-firing in cast iron woks at ~280°C (~90 seconds).
6. **Rolling (揉捻, róuniǎn):** Giving the characteristic yancha form of longitudinally twisted strands. Often done by hand.
7. **Drying and roasting (烘干/焙火, hōnggān/bèihuǒ):** Delicate multi-stage roasting over charcoal (often longan / 龙眼) at relatively low temperature (~80°C). Total duration — up to 20 hours. Two-stage roasting at moderate temperature preserves delicate floral aromas while adding only light "fire" notes.
8. **Sorting (分级, fēnjí):** Removal of stems and damaged leaves.

---

**6. Organoleptic Characteristics:**

- **Dry leaf appearance:** Large longitudinally twisted strands of dark brown or almost charcoal color with golden and reddish streaks. Leaf is dense, heavy.
- **Dry leaf aroma:** Bright, complex — rose, orchid, hyacinth; complemented by notes of caramel, honey, cedar resin and light nutty nuances.
- **Liquor aroma:** Intense, with dominance of rose and orchid (Dendrobium), shades of vanilla caramel and tropical fruits. Persistent throughout many infusions.
- **Taste:** Soft, voluminous, enveloping. Honey and fruit (peach) tones, floral sweetness, pronounced minerality (yán yùn), light astringency. Long refreshing aftertaste with citronella notes. Smooth, oily texture.
- **Liquor color:** Clear, sparkling, from golden-amber to amber-topaz with pearlescent sheen.
- **Spent leaves:** Large whole leaves of dark green color with reddish-brown edges, soft and elastic.

---

**7. Chemical Composition:**

- **Polyphenols:** ~8–15% of dry mass, including catechins (EGCG) and flavonoids. Theaflavins and thearubigins formed during oxidation.
- **Amino acids:** L-theanine (~0.8%) — content above average for Wuyi oolongs. Responsible for umami and relaxing effect.
- **Alkaloids:** Caffeine (~1.2–3%), theobromine, theophylline.
- **Minerals:** Calcium (~120 mg/100 g), magnesium (~85 mg/100 g), potassium (~55 mg/100 g), iron, manganese, fluorine — elevated content from Wuyi soils.
- **Saponins:** ~0.5%, immunomodulating properties.
- **Essential oils:** Volatile aromatic compounds, including rosocyanin (C₁₅H₁₀O₆), affecting the characteristic aftertaste.
- **Vitamins:** Groups B, C, PP.

---

**8. Health Properties:**

- **Antioxidant protection:** Polyphenols neutralize free radicals.
- **Digestive and metabolic improvement:** Activation of metabolism, fat metabolism. In TCM — normalization of "damp heat" in the stomach.
- **Tonic and adaptogenic effect:** Caffeine + L-theanine provide gentle alertness without agitation. Popular as a "cognitive stimulator" among intellectual workers.
- **Hypouricemic action:** Data on xanthine oxidase inhibition, potential reduction of uric acid levels.
- **Cardiovascular system support:** Cholesterol level normalization, vessel strengthening.
- **Immune system strengthening:** Complex of vitamins, minerals and saponins.

---

**9. Brewing:**

The gongfu tea ceremony (gōngfū chá) method is recommended for full potential revelation:

- **Teaware:** Porcelain gaiwan (preferable for aroma purity) or Yixing clay teapot, 100–150 ml.
- **Tea amount:** 5–7 g.
- **Water temperature:** 90–95°C. Soft filtered water.
- **Process:**
  1. Warm the teaware with boiling water.
  2. Add tea, pour water and immediately drain (rinse).
  3. First infusion — 10–15 seconds, then increase by 5–10 seconds.
  4. Withstands 5–8 infusions. Maximum extraction of phenolic compounds — on 3rd infusion (~25 seconds, ~92°C).
- **Western method:** 3–4 g per 200–250 ml, 90–95°C, 2–3 minutes, 2–3 steeps.
- **Tip:** Some experts recommend trying the liquor cooled to 35–40°C to perceive special aroma nuances.

---

**10. Storage:**

- Airtight opaque container (tin can, vacuum bag). Double packaging is ideal.
- Dry, cool (below 20°C), dark place, without foreign odors. Humidity — no more than 45–50%.
- Storage period: up to 24–36 months in unopened packaging. Pressed variants — longer, with development of new shades.
- Sign of spoilage: fading of floral aroma, appearance of musty, dusty or sour notes.

---

**11. Price and Counterfeits:**

Huang Mei Gui is a premium rock oolong. On the Russian market, the price for 50 g varies from 900 rubles (basic quality) to 3000–4500+ rubles for elite batches.

**How to recognize counterfeits:**
- Too low price ($5–10 for 50 g) — a sure warning signal.
- Broken leaf, dust, stems instead of whole twisted strands.
- Sharp, "perfumery" rose aroma — sign of artificial flavoring. Authentic Huang Mei Gui contains no floral additives.
- Flat, quickly fading taste with bitterness or chemical aftertastes.
- Useful authenticity marker: small charcoal particles on leaves (trace of charcoal heating) and certificates of origin (Wuyishan Tea Exchange).

---

**12. Interesting Facts:**

- The name "Yellow Rose" was given to the tea exclusively for its aroma — without adding flowers.
- Second place in the "Best Aroma" category at Tea Masters Cup 2019.
- One of the few Wuyi oolongs with halal certification.
- Tea extract is used in cosmetology (masks for problem skin) and in dietary supplement production.
- Released in several forms: classic loose leaf, pressed into cakes (~200 g), bottled cold tea, extract in capsules.
- Popular among audiophiles as a drink that helps perceive sound nuances more subtly.

---

**13. Place Among Yancha:**

Huang Mei Gui occupies an intermediate position in the Wuyi rock tea system: by oxidation level and roasting character it is closer to light, aromatic varieties (Shui Jin Gui, Bai Ji Guan) than to dark, intensely roasted ones (Da Hong Pao, Rou Gui). By emphasis on aromatics it may be closer to Anxi oolongs (Tie Guan Yin) than to classic yancha, while maintaining the characteristic Wuyi minerality (yán yùn).

---

**14. Possible Contraindications:**

- Individual intolerance.
- Exacerbation of gastrointestinal diseases (gastritis with high acidity, peptic ulcer, GERD).
- Urolithiasis in acute stage.
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding — limit to 1–2 cups per day.
- Hypersensitivity to caffeine (insomnia, tachycardia with excessive consumption).
- Use with caution when taking anticoagulants and MAO inhibitors.

---

**In conclusion:**

Huang Mei Gui is a bright example of how modern breeding can enrich centuries-old tradition without violating its essence. This young cultivar, born in the walls of a scientific institute and grown on terraced plantations of Banyan between the ancient rocks of Wuyi, carries both innovative spirit and authentic "rock character." Its unique rose-honey aroma, soft multifaceted taste and mineral depth of aftertaste make Huang Mei Gui one of the most interesting discoveries in the yancha world of recent decades.
